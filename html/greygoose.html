Grey Goose

This is a premium brand vodka that was produced in France. The original founder is named Sidney Frank before it originally went on sale in 2004 to Bacardi. The Maitre de Chai (cellar master) for Grey Goose is named Francois Thibault, who was the one who had developed the original recipe for the vodka in Cognac, France.

In Grey Goose, the wheat that is used is primarily grown in Picardy, France and is also distilled in the same region. The distillate is later on sent to Cognac, France where I has to be mixed with spring water and bottled. The soft winter wheat used in Grey Goose was sown in October and later harvested in August. This wheat is in a category named as "superior bread-making wheat".

Fun Fact

Grey Goose is entirely wheat based while many other commercial vodkas use grapes and potatoes or rye
